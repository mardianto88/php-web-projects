Caesar Cipher Encryption

What is Caesar Cipher?

The Caesar Cipher is a substitution cipher where each letter in the plaintext is shifted a certain number of places down or up the alphabet. Named after Julius Caesar, who used this encryption method in his private correspondence, it is one of the simplest and oldest encryption techniques.

How It Works:

  • Choose a shift value (key). For example, 3.
  • Shift each letter in the plaintext by the key value. For instance, with a key of 3, A becomes D, B becomes E, and so on.
  • The result is the ciphertext.
